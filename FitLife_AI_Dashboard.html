🎯 Mission & Strategic Objective

FitLife AI aims to become India's leading AI-powered health companion — a single intelligent platform unifying workout planning, nutrition coaching, recovery optimisation, and progress analytics, personalised to each user's body, goals, and Indian lifestyle.


Core strategic pillars:

  • Personalisation at scale — AI that learns and adapts continuously
  • Cultural relevance — built natively for Indian diets and price sensitivity
  • Holistic coverage — fitness + nutrition + recovery + mental wellness
  • Measurable results — every recommendation tied to trackable outcomes
  • 20%+ YoY growth — subscriptions, B2B wellness, and premium tiers

🔧 Cleaning Steps Applied
1
Income Imputation
›

q4_monthly_income — 124 missing values (~6.2%) imputed using the mode income band within each city tier group. Metro respondents skipping income are more likely to be higher earners; Tier 3 respondents more likely lower. Group-wise mode preserves this geographic income structure better than global imputation. This avoids sampling bias that row deletion would introduce.

2
Health Condition Zero-Fill
›

All 7 q16_health_* binary columns — 31 NaN values per column (~1.6%) filled with 0 (condition not present). Given these are binary flags and the skip behaviour is driven by privacy concerns rather than condition presence, zero-imputation is the correct conservative assumption.

3
Ordinal Score Encoding
›

8 ordinal categorical variables (income, WTP, activity, tech adoption, junk food frequency, comfort, activity level, activity 4-week) encoded as integer scores 1–5 to enable correlation analysis and regression modelling. Original string columns are preserved alongside scores.

4
WTP Gap Feature Engineering
›

A new column wtp_gap = wtp_score − spend_score is engineered. Positive values indicate aspirational users (want to spend more than they currently do). This gap variable is one of the strongest predictors of subscription conversion and is used in regression models.

5
Noise Row Flagging
›

Rows where q7_activity_level is 'Highly active' or 'Athlete' BUT q2_activity_last_4weeks is '0' or '1–4 times' are flagged as is_noise_row = 1 (~80 rows, ~4%). These reflect social desirability bias. Flagged but retained — they test model robustness and represent a real population phenomenon.

6
Outlier Row Flagging
›

Rows with WTP of '₹1,000+/month' despite current spend of ₹0 or ₹1–500 are flagged as is_wtp_outlier = 1 (~40 rows, ~2%). These aspirational responders inflate WTP estimates. Flagging enables Winsorisation or sensitivity analysis during regression modelling.

7
Intent Score Encoding
›

Target variable q25_subscription_intent encoded as intent_score: Not Interested=0, Undecided=1, Interested=2. Enables ordinal regression treatment and correlation analysis in addition to the standard 3-class classification framing.

⚠️ Noise & Outlier Flags

Activity Inconsistency Noise — by Activity Level

Noise pattern: Noise rows concentrate in 'Highly active' and 'Athlete' bands — social desirability bias is strongest where self-report inflation is most common. Q2 (behavioural anchor — actual activity last 4 weeks) is more reliable than Q7 (self-reported label) for training classification models.

WTP Outliers — Aspirational Spenders

Outlier pattern: Aspirational outliers appear in the '₹1,000+/month' WTP band despite ₹0–₹500 current spend — a real Indian survey phenomenon (aspirational self-presentation). Winsorise at 95th percentile or report with/without outliers for WTP regression sensitivity testing.

🧠 Motivation Types & Primary Drivers

Primary Motivation Type (Psychographic)

Actual Activity (Last 4 Weeks) × Subscription Intent

Motivation: 'Energy and health' (intrinsic) and 'Look better/confident' (extrinsic) lead. Intrinsically motivated users have significantly lower churn rates in health psychology research — this segment should be prioritised for premium conversion campaigns. Activity × Intent: A clear positive monotonic relationship — as actual exercise frequency increases, 'Interested' responses grow proportionally. Respondents exercising 9+ times in 4 weeks are 3–4× more likely to be 'Interested' than sedentary respondents. This validates Q2 as the single most important classification feature in the dataset.
📈 Self-Efficacy Score by Persona

Average Self-Efficacy Score by Persona (1 = Low Confidence, 5 = High Confidence)

Self-efficacy: Fitness Enthusiasts score highest (avg ~4.3), Sedentary Skeptics lowest (avg ~1.9). Self-efficacy is the most validated predictor of long-term fitness adherence in academic literature (Bandura, 1997). The wide inter-persona gap confirms it's a powerful clustering and classification feature — and the primary reason Sedentary Skeptics are unlikely to convert without an accountability-first product experience.
